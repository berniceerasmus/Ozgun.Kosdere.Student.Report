The Persuasion Framework

Claim → Evidence → Benefit → Address Objection → Call to Action

8 MIN▼

// THE 5-STEP PERSUASION STRUCTURE

Every successful argument follows this pattern. Use it in meetings, emails, and presentations.

1. CLAIM → state your position clearly
"I strongly believe we should change the compressor supplier."
2. EVIDENCE → support with data or facts
"The current supplier has caused three delays this year, totalling 8 weeks of lost time."
3. BENEFIT → explain the advantage
"Switching suppliers would reduce our risk and save approximately 1.2M TL in Q3."
4. ADDRESS OBJECTION → show you understand concerns
"I understand the transition requires time, however the long-term cost of staying is higher."
5. CALL TO ACTION → ask for a specific decision
"I would like to request approval to evaluate three alternative suppliers by end of month."
Key insight for Özgün: You already have the 9-year experience. The missing piece is translating that knowledge into English arguments. Numbers + benefits + alternatives = the language of decisions.

Persuasion Phrases — The Full Toolkit

Claims, evidence, benefits, objections, and calls to action

7 MIN▼
Making Your Claim
I strongly believe that…
States a firm opinion. Use when you are confident in your position.
The evidence clearly shows…
Leads with data. Immediately positions you as objective.
Based on my analysis…
Professional framing. Signals you have done your homework.
I would like to propose…
Softer claim. Good for suggestions to senior management.
Presenting Evidence
According to the data…
References objective information. Very powerful in technical contexts.
The figures indicate that…
Use with budget or performance numbers.
As the report shows…
References a document. Good for formal presentations.
In my nine years of experience…
Özgün's strongest card. Experience = credibility.
Stating Benefits
This would result in…
Connects action to outcome. Always follow with numbers if possible.
The key advantage is…
Highlights the main benefit before listing others.
Not only… but also…
Shows multiple benefits. Doubles the persuasive impact.
This would save us approximately…
Money or time savings. The most convincing benefit for a director.
Handling Objections
I understand your concern, however…
Acknowledge before disagreeing. Never dismiss. Always "however", never "but" alone.
While I appreciate that…, the data suggests…
Concedes a point before presenting a stronger counter-argument.
That is a valid point. In addition…
Agrees first, then adds your argument. Feels collaborative, not confrontational.
The risk of not acting is…
Reframes inaction as the real risk. Very effective.
Calls to Action
I would like to request approval for…
Formal approval request. Specific and professional.
I recommend that we…
Strong recommendation. Use when you are the expert in the room.
Could we agree to…?
Collaborative close. Invites agreement rather than demanding it.
The next step I propose is…
Ends with a specific action. Never leave a persuasion without a clear next step.
