Grammar — Explaining Problems Professionally

Cause and effect, passive for problems, diplomatic language

8 MIN▼

// DESCRIBING PROBLEMS — CAUSE AND EFFECT

When something goes wrong, explain the cause and the effect clearly. Use these linking structures:

due to / as a result of / because of + noun phrase
✓ The delay occurred due to a customs inspection failure.
✓ As a result of the supplier error, we lost three weeks.
This resulted in / This led to / This caused + noun
✓ The equipment failure resulted in a 2-week production shutdown.
✓ The procurement delay led to a 15% budget overrun.
Passive for problems → less blame, more professional
✓ The timeline was affected by an unforeseen supplier issue.
✗ The supplier destroyed our timeline. — too aggressive

// PROPOSING SOLUTIONS

I suggest / I recommend / I propose + -ing or that + clause
✓ I suggest contacting an emergency supplier immediately.
✓ I recommend that we escalate this to the director today.
One option would be to… / An alternative approach is to…
✓ One option would be to split the project into two phases.
✓ An alternative approach would be to renegotiate the contract terms.
Key skill: Always present problems with a solution ready. "There is a problem — and here is what I suggest." This is the language that marks senior managers. Never present a problem without a next step.

Problem-Solving Phrase Set

Identify issues, explain causes, propose solutions, manage emotions

6 MIN▼
Identifying the Problem
We have identified an issue with…
Professional opener. Signals you are in control of the situation.
It has come to our attention that…
Formal. Used in written reports or formal meetings.
The root cause of this issue is…
Shows analytical thinking. Goes beyond symptoms to causes.
This problem stems from…
Explains origin. "stems from" = comes from / originates in.
Proposing Solutions
I suggest that we…
Direct recommendation. Use when you have a clear answer.
One option would be to…
Softer suggestion. Implies there are other options too.
A possible solution is…
Open and collaborative. Good for team discussions.
To resolve this, we could…
Action-oriented. Moves directly from problem to action.
Managing Difficult Conversations
Let me be transparent about this.
Signals honest communication. Builds trust immediately.
I take full responsibility for…
Leadership language. Own the problem, then fix it.
This is not ideal, however…
Acknowledges the problem while pivoting to solutions.
What I can assure you is…
Gives confidence during uncertainty. Very powerful.
