Section 1 — What These Words Are Called

Understand the grammar name and the communication purpose.

FOUNDATION▼

THE NAME

Words such as although, however, therefore, thus, because, in addition, as a result, on the other hand are commonly called linking words, connectors, or discourse markers.

They connect ideas.
They show logic.
They make speech and writing sound structured.
Without connectors: simple but disconnected. With connectors: more professional and easier to follow.
without connectors
We have delays. The supplier is slow. We need a new plan.
with connectors
We have delays because the supplier is slow; therefore, we need a new plan.
Manager benefit: connectors do not only improve grammar. They help a manager explain cause, contrast, result, sequence, and decisions clearly in a meeting.
🗂️

Section 2 — Main Connector Families

See the main types and what each type does.

MAP▼
Contrast
although, even though, however, but, on the other hand, whereas
Result / Consequence
therefore, thus, so, as a result, consequently
Cause / Reason
because, since, as, due to, because of
Addition
and, also, in addition, moreover, furthermore
Sequence
first, then, next, after that, finally
Condition
if, unless, provided that, as long as
Clarification / Emphasis
in fact, actually, especially, in particular
Comparison
similarly, likewise, compared with, in contrast
Simple rule: first choose the logic you need. Then choose the connector. Do you want to show contrast, result, reason, or sequence?

Section 3 — Contrast Connectors

Show two ideas that do not match or point in different directions.

ALTHOUGH · HOWEVER · BUT▼

Although / Even though / But / However / On the other hand / Whereas

contrast family
B1 USE

but and although are the most useful starting points.

But is simple and very common.
We planned carefully, but we still had delays.

Although joins two ideas in one sentence.
Although we planned carefully, we still had delays.

Use although inside one sentence. It needs two parts of a sentence.
B2 UPGRADE

However usually starts a new sentence.
We planned carefully. However, we still had delays.

On the other hand compares options.
This supplier is cheaper. On the other hand, delivery is slower.

Whereas contrasts two facts more formally.
The packaging team improved output, whereas the shipping team did not.

common mistake
Although we had delays. We finished the order.
correct
Although we had delays, we finished the order.
Colleague
We added more staff, so the process should be faster now.
Özgün · B1
We added more staff, but the delays are still there.
Özgün · B2
We added more staff. However, the delays are still there, so we need to look at the process itself.
➡️

Section 4 — Result and Consequence

Show what happened because of a previous fact or problem.

THEREFORE · THUS · AS A RESULT▼

So / Therefore / Thus / As a result / Consequently

result family
B1 USE

So is the easiest result connector.
The supplier was late, so we missed the deadline.

As a result is clear and useful in both speaking and writing.
Production stopped. As a result, delivery was delayed.

B2 UPGRADE

Therefore sounds more formal and logical.
We have repeated delays; therefore, we need a new process.

Thus is even more formal and is more common in reports or formal comments.
The current workflow is inefficient; thus, changes are required.

Consequently is strong and formal.
The quality checks were skipped. Consequently, defects increased.

Sentence + therefore, + result
Sentence. Therefore, + result.
Sentence. As a result, + result.
Do not forget punctuation.
common mistake
We had delays therefore we changed the schedule.
better
We had delays; therefore, we changed the schedule.
Özgün · B1
The supplier was late, so the line stopped for two hours.
Özgün · B2
The supplier was late; therefore, the line stopped for two hours.
🔍

Section 5 — Cause and Reason

Explain why something happened or why a decision is needed.

BECAUSE · SINCE · DUE TO▼

Because / Since / As / Because of / Due to

reason family
B1 USE

Because is the most important reason word.
We changed the schedule because the delivery was late.

Because of comes before a noun or noun phrase.
We changed the schedule because of the delay.

B2 UPGRADE

Since and as can mean “because” in more formal contexts.
Since we have limited time, we need to decide today.

Due to is more formal and also comes before a noun phrase.
Production stopped due to a machine failure.

wrong pattern
Due to we had delays, we changed the plan.
correct
Due to the delays, we changed the plan.
Important pattern: because / since / as + sentence. Because of / due to + noun phrase.

Section 6 — Addition, Sequence, and Condition

Add ideas, organise steps, and explain conditions professionally.

MOREOVER · FINALLY · PROVIDED THAT▼

Addition

also · in addition · moreover · furthermore
B1 USE

Use and and also first.
We need faster delivery. We also need better communication.

B2 UPGRADE

Use in addition, moreover, or furthermore in formal meetings, emails, and reports.
Costs are increasing. Moreover, quality is not improving.

Sequence

first · then · next · finally
B1 USE

First, then, finally are enough to sound organised.
First, let’s review the issue. Then, we’ll discuss solutions. Finally, we’ll assign actions.

B2 UPGRADE

Add more variety with after that, subsequently, or in the next stage when appropriate.

Condition

if · unless · provided that · as long as
B1 USE

If is the most useful condition connector.
If we change the supplier, we may reduce delays.

B2 UPGRADE

Unless means “if not”.
We will miss the target unless we act today.

Provided that and as long as are more formal.
We can approve the plan provided that quality remains stable.

Section 7 — Work Meeting, Email, and Report Practice

See how the same connector changes across situations.

REAL CONTEXT▼
Although
Meeting: Although we increased staff, output did not improve.
However
Meeting: We expected improvement. However, the delays continued.
Therefore
Report: The process is inefficient; therefore, it should be revised.
Thus
Report: Supplier response times remain slow; thus, planning accuracy is affected.
In addition
Email: In addition, please share the updated timeline by 3 p.m.
As a result
Meeting: The machine stopped unexpectedly. As a result, production was delayed.
On the other hand
Meeting: This option is cheaper. On the other hand, it is slower.
Provided that
Email/Meeting: We can approve the change provided that quality stays stable.
Manager meeting
Although we solved the supplier issue, delivery is still unstable. Therefore, we need to review the internal process. In addition, I’d like the packaging team to share an updated timeline by tomorrow.
Email example
Dear team, the shipment arrived later than expected. As a result, tomorrow’s schedule needs to be adjusted. In addition, please confirm whether the revised plan is realistic. We can proceed with the change provided that all departments agree on the timeline.
Report example
The current packaging stage creates a delay of approximately 20 minutes per batch; therefore, output remains below target. Labour support was increased last month. However, performance did not improve significantly. Thus, a process redesign should be considered.
