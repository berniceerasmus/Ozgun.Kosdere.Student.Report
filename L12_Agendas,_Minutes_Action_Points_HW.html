Part 2 — Multiple Choice: Best Professional Response

Choose the option that sounds most professional in a work meeting.

10 MINAUTO-CHECK
▼
1) A colleague raises a topic that is not on the agenda. What is the best response?
The meeting has started and you are on item one.
Correct — acknowledging the idea and redirecting it to AOB is professional and keeps the meeting on track.
2) Which action point is written correctly?
Remember: every action point needs what, who, and when.
Exactly — what (contact supplier, confirm date), who (Ahmet), when (Thursday 5 p.m.). All three elements are present.
3) Which is correct minutes language?
Minutes use past tense and third person — reported style.
Correct — "it was agreed that [Name] would..." is the standard pattern for minutes. Past tense, third person, passive.
4) You want to check that everyone agrees before moving on. What do you say?
Exactly — "Can I just check we're all aligned?" invites confirmation actively. Assuming agreement without checking is a common meeting mistake.
5) A deadline has passed and the action was not done. Which follow-up is most professional?
Correct — this is firm, specific, and professional. It names the issue, explains the impact, and proposes a solution — without being aggressive.
